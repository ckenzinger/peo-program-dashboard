The "Why": Our Opportunity

Our current state presents clear challenges with processes, data, and reactivity. This program is our opportunity to evolve by standardizing our approach, harnessing data for insights, and proactively managing risk before incidents occur.

Standardize for Flawless Execution

By creating a single, standardized playbook, we move from fragmented processes to a unified system where everyone can execute their roles with clarity and confidence.

Harness Data for Actionable Insights

We will establish a single source of truth for incident data. This replaces unreliable information with automated, trustworthy metrics to drive our decisions.

Shift to Proactive Risk Mitigation

Instead of reacting to fires, we will use trend analysis and data insights to identify systemic issues and engage partners to improve reliability before incidents happen.

Our Ways of Working

Success depends on effective collaboration. This section outlines the core team structure, our communication cadence, and the tools we'll use to stay aligned.

The Team

A cross-functional virtual team of PEs, TPaMs, and reps from IRM, Eng, & Data Science, led by the Program Lead and Leadership Sponsor.

Our Cadence

Weekly 30-minute syncs for the core team to maintain momentum, and bi-weekly updates to Leadership to ensure alignment.

Our Tools

A central repository for all documentation and a dedicated Slack channel will serve as our hubs for information and communication.

The Path Forward: Our Work Plan

We will execute this program in four distinct phases. Click on each phase below to see the key activities and focus for that period.

Phase 1: Foundation & Discovery (2 Weeks)

The goal of this initial phase is to establish a solid foundation. We will finalize the program scope, perform a deep-dive analysis of historical incident data, and map out our current-state processes to identify key pain points.

Phase 2: Design & Documentation (4 Weeks)

With our foundation in place, we will design the future-state process. This includes defining clear roles and responsibilities, creating the v2 runbook, and specifying the requirements for our success metrics dashboard.

Phase 3: Implementation & Go-Live (4 Weeks)

This is where the plan becomes reality. We will build the necessary tools and dashboards, conduct comprehensive training for all teams on the new processes, and officially launch the new incident response program.

Phase 4: Embed & Iterate (Ongoing)

Following the launch, our focus shifts to continuous improvement. We will monitor program adoption, measure our success against the defined metrics, gather feedback, and make iterative enhancements to the process and tools.
