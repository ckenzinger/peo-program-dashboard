The “Why”: Our Opportunity

Our current incident response model has served us to this point, but as we scale, we have a clear opportunity to evolve. By addressing our current state, we can unlock significant improvements in efficiency, data quality, and proactive partner management.

The Challenge:

Our processes are fragmented and inconsistent across teams, leading to increased manual toil and impacting the quality of incident resolution.

The Opportunity:

To create a single, standardized playbook that ensures everyone knows their role and can execute flawlessly, reducing our time to mitigation.

The Challenge:

We struggle with unreliable data. Our current labels lack the granularity to distinguish between key incident types (like Stripe-induced, partner-facing) or to capture critical details like impacted partner functionality.

The Opportunity:

To drive targeted enhancements to our BRB tooling, the widely-adopted source of truth for incidents. By improving data capture within BRB, we can empower teams with reliable insights to identify trends, measure performance, and make data-driven decisions.

The Challenge:

We are often in a reactive mode, fighting fires as they arise rather than preventing them.

The Opportunity:

To shift to a proactive stance on risk. By leveraging our data, we can identify systemic issues and engage partners to improve their reliability before they cause major incidents.

The “What”: Program Vision & Goals

Our Vision

To create a clear, efficient, and reliable process framework for supporting the IRM team, reducing incident response toil by ensuring partner engagement is swift, consistent, and effective.

Establish a Centralized Process

Develop a single, comprehensive, and well-documented process for PE&O incident support. This process must be clearly communicated to all cross-functional partners and supported by a robust on-call coverage model.

Clarify Cross-Functional Roles

Define and document a clear RACI that outlines the specific responsibilities and handoffs between PE&O, IRM, Engineering, TechOps, and PDMs during an incident.

Enable Data-Driven Decisions

Replace time-consuming manual reporting with automated dashboards. We will leverage improved data from BRB to provide reliable insights into partner performance, enabling more effective internal reviews and data-backed partner conversations.

Drive Proactive Risk Reduction

Leverage incident data to move beyond reactive fire-fighting. We will proactively identify systemic issues and trends, and engage partners through formal improvement plans and readiness reviews to prevent future incidents.

Defining Success: Our Approach to Measurement

Our primary goal in this first phase is to build the foundation for measurement. Instead of setting arbitrary targets, we will focus on establishing clear metrics and improving data quality. Success will be measured by our ability to answer critical questions we can't answer today.

Establish Baselines for Key Timelines

We will track **Time to Acknowledge (TTA)** and **Time to Notify (TTN)** for incidents with user-facing impact. Success is establishing our first-ever reliable baseline averages for these metrics.

Achieve High-Quality Data Capture

Success is a three-step process: launching the **BRB enhancements**, seeing >90% **accurate utilization** of new fields, and unlocking insights with the **Hubble v2.0 dashboard**.

Validate Cross-Functional Clarity and Adoption

Success is twofold: achieving **>80% utilization of specific partner runbooks** where applicable, and receiving **positive qualitative feedback** from IRM, Eng, and TechOps on the new, clearer process.

Ensure Comprehensive Team Enablement and Readiness

Success is when every PE&O team member can articulate the program's **strategic alignment** with SOKRs and understands their **specific responsibilities**, from runbook testing to partner communication support.

Our Ways of Working

Our team's strength is its global presence. To turn our time zone differences into an advantage, we will adopt a deliberate, asynchronous-first approach. This ensures work progresses 24/7 and every team member can contribute effectively.

Principle 1: Async by Default

How we work:

  • Compass is our PM tool for milestones & status.
  • Google Drive is for all collaborative work.
  • Slack is for urgent coordination & weekly updates (not decisions).
  • Recorded videos will be used for complex demos.

Principle 2: High-Context Communication

Since team members will pick up work as others sign off, every update and comment must be clear and contain full context. Avoid "Done," prefer "Updated the RACI with the new workflow for your review, @Jane."

Principle 3: Deliberate Cadence

How we meet:

  • Core sync is weekly for the first ~6 weeks, then bi-weekly.
  • We will frequently rotate meeting times to share the time zone burden.
  • Mandatory written updates in Slack every Friday.
  • Regular updates to leadership as needed.
