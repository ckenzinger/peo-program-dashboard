The “Why”: Our Opportunity

Our current model has served us well, but as we scale, we can unlock significant improvements in efficiency, data quality, and proactive partner management.

The Challenge:

Our processes are fragmented and inconsistent across teams, leading to increased manual toil and impacting the quality of incident resolution.

The Opportunity:

To create a single, standardized playbook that ensures everyone knows their role and can execute flawlessly.

The Challenge:

We struggle with unreliable data. Our current labels lack the granularity to distinguish between key incident types (like Stripe-induced, partner-facing) or capture critical details.

The Opportunity:

To drive targeted enhancements to our BRB tooling to empower teams with reliable, data-driven insights.

The Challenge:

We are often in a reactive mode, fighting fires as they arise rather than preventing them.

The Opportunity:

To shift to a proactive stance on risk by leveraging data to identify systemic issues and improve partner reliability.

The “What”: Program Vision & Goals

Our Vision

To create a clear, efficient, and reliable process framework for supporting the IRM team, reducing incident response toil by ensuring partner engagement is swift, consistent, and effective.

Establish a Centralized Process

Develop a single, comprehensive, and well-documented process for PE&O incident support, communicated to all partners and supported by a robust on-call coverage model.

Clarify Cross-Functional Roles

Define and document a clear RACI outlining responsibilities and handoffs between PE&O, IRM, Engineering, TechOps, and PDMs during an incident.

Enable Data-Driven Decisions

Replace manual reporting with automated dashboards, leveraging improved BRB data to provide reliable insights into partner performance.

Drive Proactive Risk Reduction

Leverage incident data to move beyond reactive fire-fighting and proactively identify systemic issues, engaging partners with formal improvement plans.

Defining Success: Our Approach

Our primary goal is to build the foundation for measurement. Success will be measured by our ability to answer critical questions we can't answer today.

Establish Baselines for Key Timelines

Track TTA & TTN to establish our first reliable baseline averages.

Achieve High-Quality Data Capture

Success is launching BRB enhancements, seeing >90% accurate utilization, and unlocking insights with the Hubble v2.0 dashboard.

Validate Cross-Functional Clarity

Success is >80% runbook utilization where applicable, and positive qualitative feedback from IRM, Eng, and TechOps.

Ensure Team Enablement

Success is when every PE&O team member can articulate the program's strategic alignment and their specific responsibilities.

Our Ways of Working

To turn our global team's time zone differences into an advantage, we will adopt a deliberate, asynchronous-first approach.

Principle 1: Async by Default

How we work:

  • Compass is our PM tool for milestones & status.
  • Google Drive is for all collaborative work.
  • Slack is for urgent coordination & weekly updates.
  • Recorded videos will be used for complex demos.

Principle 2: High-Context Communication

Since team members will pick up work as others sign off, every update must be clear and contain full context. Avoid "Done," prefer "Updated the RACI with the new workflow for your review, @Jane."

Principle 3: Deliberate Cadence

How we meet:

  • Core sync is weekly for first ~6 weeks, then bi-weekly.
  • We will frequently rotate meeting times to share the time zone burden fairly.
  • Mandatory written updates in Slack every Friday.
  • Regular updates to leadership as needed.
